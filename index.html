Greek

Gorgon
- A half-woman, half snake creature how can turn people into stone.
Cerberus
- The three headed dog that guards the Underworld.
Harpie
- A winged creature that is half-bird and half-woman.
Chimera
- A fire-breathing she-monster in Greek mythology having a lion's head, a goat's body, and a serpent's tail.
Minotaur
- A monster with the head of a bull and the body of a man.

Norse

Nidhogg
- A dragon or serpent taking up residence at the roots of the cosmic world tree, Yggdrasil.
Jörmungandr
- Also known as the Midgard Serpent, is depicted as a colossal serpent that encircles the world.
Gullinbursti
- Freyr’s Golden Boar
Fenrir
- The giant wolf of Ragnarök
Kelpie
- Is a shape-shifting spirit, described as a grey or white horse-like creature, able to adopt human form.

Egyptain

The Sphinx
- A creature with the head of a human and the body of a lion.
Serpopard
- The creature is a combination of a serpent and a leopard, with the body of a leopard and the long neck and head of a serpent
Ammit
- A creature with the head of a crocodile, the torso of a lioness or leopard, and the lower body and hind legs of a hippopotamus
Apophis
- A demon of chaos, who had the form of a serpent and, as the foe of the sun god, Re.
Bennu
- Depicted as a heron or a phoenix, that represents creation, rebirth, and the cyclical nature of life and death.

Japanese

Kitsune
- Shapeshifting spirits resembling foxes, sometimes with multiple tails.
Bakeneko
- Are monster cats that have lived long enough to become yokai.
Gashadokuro
- A mythical creature known as a Giant Skeleton Yokai
Kappa
- A humanoid figure with the shell of a tortoise on its back, long shaggy hair, and a beak for a mouth.
Nure-onna
- A reptilian creature with the head of a woman and the body of a snake.

Dragons

What do you think of when you think “Dragon”? A large beast with wings? A scaly monster that’ll eat you as soon as it looks at you. What if I told you dragons are more than a creature that lurks in cave and guards large piles of gold. The dragon has been around for a long time and appeared in texts all around the world. Let’s take a deep dive into how dragons have influenced ancient culture and modern storytelling.
